Second Talk:

When: 17th Feb 2012, 11 am

Where: ACES 3.408

Speaker: Pravesh Kothari

Title: The Sum Product Theorem and its Applications

Abstract: The sum product theorem is a basic result in additive combinatorics with a variety of surprising applications. Simply stated, it states that over a field F that satisfies simple irreducibility criteria (which is satisfied for example by R and every prime field), for any arbitrary subset A of F, either the sum-set of A ( A + A) or the product-set of A (A XA) is large. This result has surprisingly many applications in diverse areas of mathematics. In this talk, we outline the applications the sum-product theorem by discussing applications in Combinatorial Geometry, Group Theory, Randomness Extractors, Number Theory. We also plan to cover at least an outline of the proof of the Sum product theorem. Depending on the time we have, we might dive into the proof of the theorem too.
