Here are a few general guidelines for the talks/discussions in these meetings.


1. The topics could be anything that discuss recent research in theoretical computer science, machine learning, and related areas in mathematics etc. 


2. Talks that throw a few open problems for the audience to think about are encouraged.

3. The talks are expected to be of a little less formal nature than a standard 50 minute talk-10 minute questions format and the length/style of the presentation can be chosen by the speaker in order to suit the purpose.

 4. Instead of presenting all the theorems/results in a paper and making the talk information-packed, we expect the focus to be on a few results presented in enough detail so that an attentive listener can understand and appreciate the proof technique.
